Helping the youth

build caring communities

Through its mentorship programme, Lemonade aims to provide the youth with the tools, guidance, and support they need to confront today’s challenges.


We would like to create a space where they can learn who they are and who they want to be, develop the skills and strengths to achieve their dreams for themselves and their community, and be inspired to champion care, kindness, and humanity.

Our Mission

We aim to help develop the youth’s skills, strengths, and values, that will allow them to build a nurturing society.
Active guidance through mentorship
Opportunities to build skills, strengths and values
A digital and print platform for knowledge exchange
A community within which to take this journey

Our Vision

A caring society where people can flourish as individuals, and contribute towards the progress of their communities.

Our Values

Care
Sustained and collective approach and efforts to uphold the wellbeing of all.
Kindness
A compassionate attitude towards people and all living beings.
Courage
The moral determination to uphold and fight for one’s values, even when it entails risks, dangers, or sacrifice.
Critical Thinking
An informed understanding of the world, especially from the perspective of the oppressed.
Humility
An attitude of selflessness and focus on what needs to be done, for the things that truly matter.
Equality
The belief that everyone is an equal and deserving of the good.
Creativity
An openness to different ideas, especially to enrich one’s own.
Growth-seeking
The aspiration for change, in the self and in the community, for the common good.

Our Programme

We have prepared a three-part core curriculum, founded on principles of collective care, that aims to build confident, competent, and most of all, compassionate youth out of each Lemonade mentorship graduate.
Part 1: Pursuing growth
Introduction to the programme Learning about myself Building the discipline for growth Models of human development The self and others
Part 2: Exploring possibilities
My communities The national situation The global situation Studying social problems Imagining a better world
Part 3: Building better communities
Creating and keeping healthy relationships Community leadership Building caring spaces Planning for better communities Community immersion
Practicum period
Each graduate of the core courses will be tasked to plan and implement a project for their community. A list of additional subjects can also be pursued, to be delivered by mentors in workshops: - Career development - The creative life - Fitness - Financial literacy: A care-based approach - Relationships - Sex education - Harm reduction - International solidarity - Community service - Sustainability - Media literacy - Gender equality

FAQs

Why did you start Lemonade?
We recognize the need for a programme that will provide the Filipino youth with the tools, guidance, and support to confront today’s challenges.

We would like to create an opportunity for them to learn who they are, believe that they can be who they want to be, and champion care, kindness, and humanity in their communities.
Why Lemonade?
We were inspired by the adage that goes, “When life hands you lemons, make lemonade.” In the face of today’s challenges, we’d like to help the youth learn how they can grow as individuals, and build caring communities.

We’d like them to always find, even in the bleakest circumstances, a space and chance to make things better.
What is the Lemonade mentorship programme?
Our mentorship programme is an initiative that aims to provide the youth with active guidance, support, and learning opportunities to become confident, competent, and compassionate individuals and members of their communities.
How do you find mentors?
For our first rollout, our mentors will be the members of our Execom, BOT, and advisory group.

Future ones will be rigorously screened by the board, according to strict standards of character, ability to work with the youth, and a track record of community service and advocacy.
